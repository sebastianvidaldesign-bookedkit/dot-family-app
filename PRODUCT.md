## Users

**Primary:** An 11-year-old girl using the app privately on her phone. She needs the UI to feel personal and cozy — not medical, not grown-up wellness. She should feel ownership and safety, never clinical judgment.

**Secondary:** Two parents (mom and dad) with optional, child-controlled read-only access. They check in occasionally, usually on mobile.

## Product Purpose

Dot is a private family cycle-tracking app. The child logs period days, flow, and symptoms. Parents see only what the child explicitly shares. No public registration, no analytics, no third-party tracking. Three accounts total.

## Brand Personality

Warm. Calm. Trustworthy. Feels like a personal diary with a gentle companion, not a medical tool. The cat mascot (simple, line-art style) makes health tracking feel safe and familiar — a cozy presence rather than a clinical interface.

**Anti-references:**
- Clue, Flo, Period Tracker — adult fertility/wellness apps
- Heavy red UI, clinical red color schemes
- Medical dashboards, charts, graphs
- Admin-style data tables
- Anything that feels like a hospital app

## Design Principles

1. The child is always in control — she owns her data and sharing settings.
2. Never make the period state feel scary or clinical. Soft colors, gentle copy.
3. Mobile-first always. Designed for a 375–430px phone screen.
4. Tap targets minimum 44px. Primary actions 64px+.
5. Cute without being babyish — the cat mascot is gentle illustration, not cartoon clip-art.
6. Copy is short, warm, and friendly. No medical jargon.
7. Privacy feels empowering and safe, not restrictive.
8. Desktop centers the app in a ~430px container — phone viewport, not wide dashboard.
